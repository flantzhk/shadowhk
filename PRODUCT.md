# ShadowHK — Product Context

**Register:** product (app UI; design serves daily practice)

**What it is:** A Cantonese learning PWA built on the shadowing method. Scene-first: users learn through real Hong Kong situations (cha chaan teng, minibus, wet market), never flashcard lists. Live at flantzhk.github.io/shadowhk.

**Users:** Adults living in or connected to Hong Kong; expats, HK-born non-speakers (the founder is one), heritage learners. Practice happens in short daily sessions: phone on the MTR, laptop at a desk. 40+ users matter: readability floor is real.

**Brand voice:** Editorial magazine, not ed-tech. "ISSUE 01" eyebrows, serif headlines, documentary photography of real HK. The city is the teacher. Warm, concrete, adult. Banned words in UI: fun, journey, fluency, easy, gamified, unlock, amazing, native-like. No em dashes.

**Anti-references:** Duolingo's cartoon gamification; generic SaaS dashboards; language-app mascots and confetti; hero-metric templates.

**Strategic principles:**
- Audio is the product; every screen serves listening and speaking.
- Real HK over idealized HK: interactions must match how the city actually works.
- Honest feedback: never fabricate scores or metrics; show what the scorer heard.
- Offline-first: a plane with no wifi is a first-class context.
